Confidence Level Definitions (CyclePath Enhancement)

This is our addition — Sensiplan is binary (shift meets rules or doesn't)
Level Criteria Badge
High 0 excluded days in reference 6. All temps recorded, no data gaps. Standard confirmation or 4th-day exception — both count as high. High confidence
Moderate 1–2 excluded days in the reference 6 (engine reached back to fill). Coverline is based on a wider window of temps. Valid evaluation, review recommended. Moderate confidence
Low 3+ excluded days in the reference window, OR significant data gaps (missing unrecorded days between reference temps). Engine could evaluate but data is sparse — user should review carefully. Low confidence
Cannot evaluate Fewer than 6 valid temps available before the potential shift. Engine cannot apply the 3-over-6 rule. No proposition generated. No card shown
What does NOT affect confidence: Which Sensiplan rule was used (standard 3-day vs 4th-day exception), how much clearance above +0.2°C, or post-shift temp stability. These are either standard method rules or separate monitoring concerns.

▶ How is the coverline calculated? (Sensiplan method)
Step 1: Identify the 6 valid temperatures immediately before the apparent shift.
Step 2: Find the highest of those 6 readings.
Step 3: The coverline is drawn at this highest value.
Step 4: To confirm a shift, 3 consecutive temperatures must be above the coverline, with the 3rd at least +0.2°C above it.
Exception: If the 3rd doesn't clear +0.2°C, a 4th consecutive temp above the coverline confirms the shift.
Engine's suggestion: Day 15, coverline 36.4°C (matches your settings)
