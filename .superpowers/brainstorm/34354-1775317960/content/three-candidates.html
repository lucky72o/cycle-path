Scenario C: The "Adjacent Candidates" Problem

When candidates are 1 day apart with the same coverline, they're essentially the same interpretation shifted by a day. Should these be merged?

Merging Adjacent Candidates
Without Merging (3 candidates)
🥇 Day 14 — Moderate (coverline 36.4°C)
🥈 Day 15 — Moderate (coverline 36.4°C)
🥉 Day 16 — Moderate (coverline 36.6°C, 4th-day)
Days 14 & 15 feel redundant — same coverline, 1 day apart
With Merging (2 distinct candidates)
🥇 Day 14-15 range — Moderate
(coverline 36.4°C, shift starts Day 14 or 15)
🥈 Day 16 — Moderate (different coverline: 36.6°C, 4th-day exception)
(fundamentally different interpretation)
Cleaner — groups similar interpretations, highlights real differences

Proposed Rule: Smart Candidate Display

Display Rules
1. Suppress low-confidence candidates
If post-shift temps drop below coverline → filter out (mention in reasoning text only)
2. Merge adjacent candidates
If two candidates are ≤2 days apart AND share the same coverline → merge into a range ("Day 14-15")
3. Show up to 3 distinct candidates
After suppression + merging, show the top 3. In practice this will almost always be 1-2.
4. Always explain why
Each candidate includes a one-line reason: "strongest clearance", "fewer excluded days", "earlier shift detected"
