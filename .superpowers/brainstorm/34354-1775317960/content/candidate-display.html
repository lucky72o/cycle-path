Option: Show Best Only
Engine shows Day 18 (high confidence). User never sees the Day 13 false start. Clean and simple.

But: if user is on Day 16 (mid-staircase), they saw a Day 13 suggestion that then disappeared. Why? They have to trust the engine.
Option: Show Runner-Up
Engine shows Day 18 as primary + "Also considered Day 13 (low confidence — temps dropped on Days 16-17)"

User understands why the engine moved from Day 13 to Day 18. Transparent reasoning.

Option: Show Best Only
Shows Day 17 only. But Day 14 is also a legitimate reading — an experienced user might prefer the earlier shift because it gives them a longer confirmed luteal phase.

The engine chose for them. Some FAM users would disagree.
Option: Show Runner-Up
Shows Day 17 as primary, Day 14 as alternative. User picks based on their understanding of their body.

Empowers user choice. Aligns with hybrid philosophy.

Scenario 4: Information Overload Risk

Messy cycle with multiple exclusions — engine finds 3-4 technically valid candidates

Proposition Card — Too Many Candidates?
⚠️ Multiple shift candidates detected
🥇 Day 19 — Confidence: Moderate (coverline 36.3°C, clearance +0.3°C)
🥈 Day 16 — Confidence: Moderate (coverline 36.2°C, clearance +0.25°C)
🥉 Day 22 — Confidence: Low (coverline 36.4°C, 4th-day exception needed)
4️⃣ Day 13 — Confidence: Low (2 excluded days in reference)
⚠ This is overwhelming. A user who needs Sensiplan guidance is unlikely to benefit from choosing between 4 candidates. This is the scenario where showing everything backfires.

Summary: The Trade-Offs

Best Only

Pros

  • Clean, simple UI
  • No decision paralysis
  • Works for beginners
  • Engine takes responsibility

Cons

  • Hides reasoning
  • Experienced users may disagree
  • Mysterious when suggestion changes
  • Less educational

Show Runner-Up

Pros

  • Transparent reasoning
  • Empowers experienced users
  • Explains engine decisions
  • Educational

Cons

  • Can overwhelm beginners
  • Messy cycles = too many options
  • More complex UI
  • Decision burden on user
